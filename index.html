How to read the plant before you pick
  1. Odorysseus is closer. That spadix, the tall center column, has colored up. Odora is still green and shut.
  2. They open late. Green Boy, The Huntington's 2025 corpse flower, unfurled on the evening of July 30 and peaked overnight around 2am. Bet evening or overnight.
  3. The window is short. A bloom lasts 24 to 48 hours, but the stink peaks about six hours after it opens and is half gone by breakfast. What you are guessing is the moment it starts.
  4. Watch the spathe. That's the outer wrap. When it pulls away and shows maroon, the bloom is hours out. Not days.
  5. The Huntington's own call: both plants bloom “in the coming weeks,” possibly overlapping, with viewing listed July 2–31, dates approximate. A window, not a day. Nobody, not even the botanists, can predict the day.
  6. Fun fact: it's not technically a single flower. It's an inflorescence — a cluster structure holding hundreds of tiny individual flowers hidden at the base, inside the spathe. Everyone calls it "the flower" anyway, us included.
